Throughout my daily readings in Matthew in Chapter 2 it really stuck out to me, 
the few things that first stuck out was that even within this chapter alone Jesus had fulfilled 3 prophecies and foretold that Jesus is the son of God through the Magi (the wise men / astrologers) who had come through the star through the land of Judea and through that they seeked to the king Herod at the time and had said there trying to find where the King of the Jews had been born and so Herod had sent out and said "go and search carefully for the child.... report to me, so that i too may go and worship him" but in reality his motivates where not pure and sense they had been warned in a dream they had not returned to Herod and in return Herod had sent to kill all the young boys under 2 years old but the part that shocked me throughout this visit is the gifts they had given them.. gold, frankincense and myrrh and the reason this was significant was because 

**First**: Frankincense was a rare and valuable resin used in ancient religious worship in the temple and a component of sacred incense burned before God and was significant symbolism of divinity and priestly intercession acknowledging and foreshadowing his role as the High Priest who would mediate between God and humanity 

and  then secondly Myrrh, it was an aromatic resin used in anointing oil for priest and sacred objects and notably in burial practices of the ancient world to anoint and preserve bodies and its prophetic because its symbolizing suffering, mortality, and death which unknowingly pointed to Jesus's sacrificial death on the cross as a suffering servant who would give his life for the redemption of humanity's sins
